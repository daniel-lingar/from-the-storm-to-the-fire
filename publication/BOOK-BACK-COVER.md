# Back Cover / Sales Copy

## Back Cover Copy

Some lives do not break all at once.

They burn slowly.

**From the Storm to the Fire: The Truth Behind the Silence** is Daniel “Bret” Lingar’s raw account of growing up inside fear, carrying unspoken trauma into adulthood, falling into addiction, losing family, failing people he loved, and finally finding the language to understand the machinery underneath the wreckage.

This is not a clean recovery story.

It does not pretend addiction was harmless. It does not erase the damage. It does not ask the reader to confuse explanation with excuse.

Instead, it asks harder questions:

What if freeze is not defiance?

What if addiction begins as relief before it becomes destruction?

What if shame keeps people sick longer than truth ever could?

What if the man everyone called broken was actually running survival code that had never been updated?

Through the images of the glass box, the blanket, the rescue boat, the second wound, and the ember that stayed, Bret turns lived experience into a map for anyone who has ever been called lazy, weak, selfish, crazy, or beyond repair.

The storm shaped him.

The fire nearly consumed him.

But the ember stayed.

And the story is not over.

## Long Storefront Description

**From the Storm to the Fire: The Truth Behind the Silence** is a raw memoir about Complex PTSD, addiction, shame, grief, fatherhood, and the long road from survival to accountability.

Daniel “Bret” Lingar writes from inside the wreckage: premature birth, childhood fear, family violence, addiction, relationship damage, legal collapse, loss, isolation, and the painful work of learning that survival patterns can become destructive when they are never understood.

This book does not excuse harm. It explains the machinery behind it.

It asks what happens when a nervous system learns danger before safety, when substances become a rescue boat, when shame becomes a second wound, and when accountability has to become something deeper than self-hatred.

For readers who have ever felt broken, misunderstood, addicted, frozen, ashamed, or trapped inside reactions they could not explain, this book offers language.

Not a cure.

Not a clinical manual.

A map.
