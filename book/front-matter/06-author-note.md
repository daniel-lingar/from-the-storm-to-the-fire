# Author’s Note

I did not write this book to make myself look innocent.

I wrote it because silence made the pattern harder to see.

For a long time, people saw the outside results: addiction, absence, collapse, lateness, shutdown, emotional distance, legal trouble, broken trust, and damage I could not undo.

Those things were real.

This book does not erase them.

But there was also machinery underneath them — fear, shame, freeze, fawning, dissociation, grief, Complex PTSD, and a body that learned survival before it learned safety.

That machinery does not excuse what happened.

It explains why repair has to go deeper than punishment, deeper than shame, and deeper than being told to “just do better.”

This is the truth behind the silence as I understand it now.
